What is Depression?

Depression is more than temporary sadness. It is a mental health condition that can affect your emotions, thoughts, physical health, relationships, work, and daily activities. People experiencing depression often feel persistent sadness, emptiness, hopelessness, low motivation, and loss of interest in activities they once enjoyed. Depression can affect anyone regardless of age, gender, or profession. With timely counselling and emotional support, many individuals experience significant improvement and regain control over their lives.

Common Symptoms of Depression

  • Persistent sadness
  • Feeling hopeless
  • Loss of motivation
  • Difficulty concentrating
  • Sleeping too much or too little
  • Changes in appetite
  • Low confidence
  • Fatigue and low energy
  • Social withdrawal
  • Feeling emotionally numb

Possible Causes

Depression may develop due to prolonged stress, relationship issues, grief, work pressure, trauma, loneliness, financial difficulties, family conflicts, or other life challenges. Sometimes multiple factors combine to affect emotional well-being.
